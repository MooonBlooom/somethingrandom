Story

When the quirky teenager Luz Noceda stumbles through a portal into the mystical Boiling Isles, she decides to stay and learn magic with wanted criminal Eda the Owl Lady and the tiny but mighty King of demons.

My Thoughts

While it starts off a bit slow (and with a first episode that doesn't really match the tone of the rest of the show), The Owl House grows into a quirky fantasy adventure with relatable and complex characters, gorgeous fight scenes, some great LGBTQ+ representation, and a beautiful message about understanding others and yourself.
